## Scoring Takeaways by Company

### Tier 1: Fortress (50+ / 80, 6+ Strong Moats)

**1. Microsoft (MSFT) — 63/80 | 8 Strong Moats**
The only company scoring 5+ across all 8 dimensions. Azure data centers give it the physical infrastructure moat that pure software companies lack, while Office 365 + LinkedIn + GitHub + Dynamics create unmatched workflow, distribution, and ecosystem lock-in — though even Microsoft's legendary distribution couldn't crush Intuit, proving no moat is absolute.

**2. Cloudflare (NET) — 54/80 | 6 Strong Moats**
A rare software company with physical infrastructure depth (330+ cities, 12K+ network interconnections). Seeing 20% of all internet traffic creates a data-network flywheel where every new customer improves threat intelligence for all others — a compounding moat AI labs cannot replicate without building their own global edge network.

**3. Intuit (INTU) — 53/80 | 7 Strong Moats**
Gokul's personal favorite example of distribution moat — his own CPA forced him onto QuickBooks. Survived Microsoft's repeated bundling attacks because its CPA network, regulatory complexity (50-state tax code), and workflow depth created non-replicable moats. The rare pure-software company scoring 7 across most dimensions.

**4. Oracle (ORCL) — 51/80 | 7 Strong Moats**
OCI's $35B+ annual capex and Stargate partnership give Oracle physical infrastructure depth rivaling hyperscalers. Decades of embedded ERP/database workflows with $350K-$500K+ migration costs create extreme switching friction. Weakest on network effects — fundamentally a vendor-customer relationship, not a platform.

### Tier 2: Secure (40-49 / 80, 4-5 Strong Moats)

**5. ServiceNow (NOW) — 46/80 | 5 Strong Moats**
Workflow is the crown jewel — 98% retention with the platform so embedded in IT/HR/CSM operations that rip-and-replace is near impossible. AI "control tower" strategy for agentic workflows deepens the moat. Weak on network effects and zero physical infrastructure.

**6. Twilio (TWLO) — 45/80 | 5 Strong Moats**
Deep API integration into engineering stacks creates painful switching, and Segment's CDP adds a proprietary customer identity graph. 10M+ developer community and 4,800 carrier connections across 180+ countries provide distribution breadth. Physical moat is limited — runs on AWS, not own telecom infrastructure.

**7. Autodesk (ADSK) — 44/80 | 5 Strong Moats**
Workflow moat is extraordinary — DWG format lock-in, decades of muscle memory, multi-year migration costs for Revit BIM models. Building proprietary "Neural CAD" foundation models on decades of 3D design data. Zero physical infrastructure and AI-eroded scale drag the total down.

**8. AppLovin (APP) — 44/80 | 5 Strong Moats**
AXON 2.0 processes 2M+ ad auctions/second with ~80% mediation market share, creating an elite data flywheel. Two-sided network effects (publishers-advertisers) are self-reinforcing. Key risk: SEC investigation and Apple/Google privacy rule changes could undermine the data advantage overnight.

**9. Figma (FIG) — 44/80 | 5 Strong Moats**
Gokul's highest-multiple angel investment (500-1000x) for good reason — multiplayer collaboration created network effects that displaced Adobe in design. 136% NDR and multi-product expansion (Sites, Slides, Make) deepen workflow entrenchment. Zero regulatory or physical moats as pure software.

**10. Salesforce (CRM) — 40/80 | 3 Strong Moats**
Gokul scored Salesforce at exactly 3 strong moats — workflow, distribution, ecosystem — and this analysis confirms it. Does NOT have scale mode (AI eroded it). Critical strategic question: must "commoditize a complement" by deciding whether the profit pool is in data or workflows.

**11. DocuSign (DOCU) — 40/80 | 5 Strong Moats**
Cross-side network effects (every envelope is a product demo) and Iris AI trained on billions of agreements provide compounding defensibility. The strategic question is whether DocuSign can migrate its massive but shallow e-signature-only customer base into deeper IAM/CLM workflows before AI commoditizes the signature layer.

**12. Workday (WDAY) — 40/80 | 4 Strong Moats**
Hire-to-retire system of record with 98% gross retention. Multi-jurisdictional payroll compliance (50-state tax, 34+ languages) creates genuine regulatory moat. Weak on network effects — your HR system doesn't get better because other companies use Workday. $5B buyback signals management confidence.

### Tier 3: Moderate (35-39 / 80, 3-5 Strong Moats)

**13. Adobe (ADBE) — 38/80 | 4 Strong Moats**
Workflow depth (Photoshop/Illustrator muscle memory) and commercially-safe Firefly training data are genuine moats. But Figma owns 80-90% of UI/UX, Canva has 200M+ users commoditizing the low end, and AI tools are making "good enough" creative output trivially accessible — stock down 43% reflecting this.

**14. Datadog (DDOG) — 38/80 | 4 Strong Moats**
20+ products with 84% multi-product adoption create deep DevOps/SRE workflow embedding. 1,000+ integrations with a commercial marketplace add ecosystem lock-in. Right at the threshold of "pretty damn secure" but faces commoditization pressure from open-source (Grafana, OpenTelemetry) and hyperscaler-native tools.

**15. UiPath (PATH) — 38/80 | 4 Strong Moats**
Thousands of embedded RPA bots create painful switching costs, and WorkFusion acquisition adds AML/KYC compliance depth. But Microsoft Power Automate can leverage Copilot to commoditize basic automation at near-zero marginal cost — the AI-eroded scale moat is UiPath's biggest vulnerability.

**16. Unity (U) — 38/80 | 5 Strong Moats**
Behavioral data from ~70% of top mobile games feeds the Vector AI ad platform, and the 2023 pricing revolt proved developers couldn't leave despite fury. Google's Genie 3 demo triggered a 24-28% crash, exposing market perception of AI disruption risk, though the workflow/ecosystem moats run deep.

**17. Snowflake (SNOW) — 37/80 | 6 Strong Moats**
Breadth but not overwhelming depth — six moats at 5+ but most sit right at the boundary. Data gravity and marketplace network effects are real but Databricks has surpassed Snowflake in revenue growth (65% vs. slower), and Apache Iceberg is reducing data lock-in. Feature convergence erodes differentiation.

**18. Dynatrace (DT) — 37/80 | 4 Strong Moats**
Smartscape topology graph + causal AI (Davis) are genuinely proprietary data assets. OneAgent auto-discovery creates deep workflow embedding with mid-90s% retention. Competes directly with Datadog for observability dominance — must keep compounding workflow depth to stay ahead.

### Tier 4: Exposed (30-34 / 80, 2-3 Strong Moats)

**19. HubSpot (HUBS) — 35/80 | 3 Strong Moats**
Inbound marketing flywheel, free CRM PLG, and 2,000+ marketplace integrations create workflow/distribution/ecosystem moats. But falls short of "pretty damn secure" — AI-native CRM startups (Attio, Day AI founded by a HubSpot co-founder) attack the mid-market with AI-first architectures.

**20. Atlassian (TEAM) — 34/80 | 3 Strong Moats**
Gokul scored Atlassian at 3 strong moats, confirmed here (workflow, ecosystem, data). Jira is the de facto engineering project management standard, but the data moat is underexploited — Gokul specifically said they "need to use that data to build products." No regulatory, physical, or scale moats.

**21. Monday.com (MNDY) — 32/80 | 3 Strong Moats**
Work OS with 200+ use cases and multi-product CRM/Dev/Service creates real switching costs, but 110% NDR suggests embedding is not as deep as leaders. "Lacks depth while chasing breadth" makes it vulnerable to AI-native tools that replicate horizontal functionality. A "compound or die" execution story.

**22. Confluent (CFLT) — 31/80 | 2 Strong Moats**
Once embedded as the real-time data backbone, switching is brutal — but the ecosystem is partially shared with Kafka-compatible competitors (Amazon MSK, Redpanda). IBM acquiring at $11B reflects that Confluent has strong product-market fit but lacks multi-dimensional fortress defensibility.

**23. MongoDB (MDB) — 30/80 | 3 Strong Moats**
Painful database migration, developer-led distribution, and wire-protocol ecosystem dominance provide moderate defensibility. But databases are not natural network-effect businesses, and AI code generation + wire-compatible alternatives (DocumentDB, FerretDB, PostgreSQL JSONB) lower switching costs.

### Tier 5: Vulnerable (<30 / 80, 0-1 Strong Moats)

**24. Zoom (ZM) — 27/80 | 1 Strong Moat**
A classic single-product company that Gokul warns "cannot be" in today's market. "Zoom" became a verb but brand is NOT a moat. Microsoft bundles Teams free with M365, erasing Zoom's distribution advantage. No data flywheel, no network effects, no physical assets, no regulatory barriers. Must compound into a platform or face decline.

**25. Asana (ASAN) — 25/80 | 2 Strong Moats**
Work Graph data model and multi-product expansion create moderate workflow stickiness, but 96% NRR reveals embedding isn't deep enough. Lacks network effects, ecosystem lock-in, or proprietary distribution to defend against Microsoft's bundling power and AI-native work management tools.

**26. Freshworks (FRSH) — 24/80 | 1 Strong Moat**
Competitively exposed with only workflow barely clearing the 5+ bar. 105-108% NRR in a market where leaders achieve 120%+ signals shallow embedding. Core ticketing/ITSM products are among the most vulnerable to AI agent replacement. Competes against Salesforce, ServiceNow, and Zendesk without differentiated data, network effects, or distribution.

**27. C3.ai (AI) — 20/80 | 1 Strong Moat**
Revenue down 46% YoY, no proprietary data flywheel, no network effects, fragile partner-dependent distribution. Hyperscalers are building native AI capabilities that render C3.ai's platform layer increasingly redundant. Well below every meaningful defensibility threshold in Gokul's framework.

## Key Framework Insights

- **Workflow + Data** are the two moats pure software companies must own. Every company scoring 40+ has at least one of these at 7+.
- **Physical Infrastructure** separates the top 4 (MSFT, NET, INTU, ORCL) from the pack. Only Microsoft, Cloudflare, and Oracle score above 3 on physical — and all three are in the top 4.
- **Network Effects** are rare in enterprise SaaS. Only Figma (8), Cloudflare (8), AppLovin (7), Microsoft (7), and Intuit (7) score 5+.
- **Brand is NOT a moat.** Zoom's brand ("it's a verb") scored it exactly 0 incremental defensibility. Gokul is right.
- **Scale is eroding.** Every pure software company acknowledged AI-driven compression of scale advantages. The median scale score is 4.5/10.
